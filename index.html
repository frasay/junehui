Team Lessons

Janay

Prioritise and protect

"The biggest lesson: the importance of prioritisation and clarity. Be clear on what matters most, hold boundaries around scope, and help the business focus on the communications that will have the most impact, rather than trying to do everything."

Kelley

Audience and strategy first

"Know your audience. Pre-aligning, pre-briefing, and building in feedback always pays off. Start with our strategy. When work clearly delivers to our strategy, the path is smooth."

Cathy

Listen, connect, learn

"Listen to the people who know. East Gore is a great example of a school working alongside its council and community, with tamariki at the centre. The result: happy, active kids and whānau, and a stronger community."

Jewels

Just do it

"You only live once. If you want to do something, don't wait. Just do it."

Maylene

Cultural competency is foundational

"To effectively partner with Māori communities, we need increased cultural competency, knowledge, confidence, and empathy. This supports our values, our commitment to Te Tiriti, and future external work."

Ann-Marie

Patience, resilience, and a bit of nagging

"Patience for processes, resilience for the ever-evolving AI landscape, upskilling in managing agency relationships, and assertive nagging is actually a very valuable skill."

Jos

Use the channels that work

"Raelene's LinkedIn posts on sport diplomacy averaged 600 likes. Explore using her channels more to amplify our reach."

Simon R

NSOs want to collaborate

"Say it Proud conversations revealed a strong appetite from NSOs to partner with us on content creation, reaching shared audiences, and tackling common fan engagement challenges. This is already shaping the evolution of the HPSNZ Marcomms framework."

Christine

Collaboration and commitment create magic

"Cross-team projects with multiple objectives and fluid timelines are hard. What made the difference: being highly collaborative, staying transparent, and keeping Alicia at the centre of every decision. When an idea sparks and the right people commit to seeing it through, magic happens."

Kylie

Connect to the emotion

"You can't make a decision unless you connect to an emotion. This is helpful in both writing comms and interpersonal relationships."

Guy

Light it right

"Shooting Ken Martell taught me that direct lighting isn't as good as 45-degree off-centre lighting to add depth to a shot. Small details make a big difference in visual storytelling."
